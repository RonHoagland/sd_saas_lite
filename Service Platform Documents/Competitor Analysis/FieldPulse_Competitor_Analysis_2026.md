# Executive Summary

FieldPulse is our closest direct competitor in the field service management space, positioned between entry-level tools (Jobber, Housecall Pro) and enterprise solutions (ServiceTitan). They target the same core market ServizmaDesk aims for: small to mid-sized field service businesses (5-200 employees) in HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and related trades.

**Key Findings:**
- **Target Market:** 5-200 employees (overlaps perfectly with ServizmaDesk)
- **Pricing:** $99-$399/month estimated (lacks transparency — requires sales call)
- **Positioning:** "Built to scale" — emphasizes customizability and workflow flexibility
- **Core Strength:** Custom workflows and project management for multi-day jobs
- **Core Weakness:** Opaque pricing, buggy QuickBooks integration, limited automation

**Strategic Implication:** FieldPulse occupies the exact middle ground ServizmaDesk is targeting. Their weaknesses (pricing transparency, QuickBooks issues, lack of asset-centric design) are our opportunities.

# 4. Technical Architecture

## 4.1 Platform & Deployment

- **Deployment:** Cloud-native SaaS
- **Access:** Web browser (desktop) + mobile apps (iOS, Android)
- **No on-premise installation required**
- **Multi-device sync** — Real-time across web, mobile, tablet

## 4.2 Data Model (Inferred)

**Job-Centric Architecture** (not asset-centric like ServizmaDesk)

Primary flow:
1. Customer
2. Job/Work Order
3. Estimate → Invoice
4. Payment

Equipment/assets are tracked *within* jobs but are not the central organizing principle.

**Key Difference vs. ServizmaDesk:**
- FieldPulse: Job → Equipment (optional link)
- ServizmaDesk: Asset (equipment) → Job (structured around the asset)

## 4.3 Offline Capabilities

- **Claimed:** Offline mode for mobile app
- **Reality (User Reports):** "Unreliable offline mode causing data loss"
- Users specifically mention issues in areas with poor cell coverage
- Recommendation: Don't rely on offline mode for critical data entry
